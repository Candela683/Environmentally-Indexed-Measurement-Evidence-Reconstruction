You are screening publications for inclusion in a database of field-derived arsenic measurements in marine organisms.

Task:
Determine whether the publication should be INCLUDED or EXCLUDED according to the screening criteria below.

Exclusion criteria:
(1) Review articles, editorials, conference abstracts, or methodological studies without primary biological measurements.
(2) Studies that do not involve marine organisms.
(3) Studies reporting only environmental media (e.g., seawater, sediment, suspended particles, pore water) without organism-associated measurements.
(4) Laboratory exposure, culture, feeding, uptake, depuration, toxicity, or bioassay experiments rather than field-collected marine organisms.
(5) Commercial seafood, market products, processed food, imported products, or retail samples without traceable field sampling locations.
(6) Qualitative descriptions without extractable concentration measurements.
(7) Measurements that cannot be linked to a biological sample, tissue, matrix, species, genus, or taxonomic group.
(8) Insufficient information for record reconstruction, including ambiguous organism identity, measurement basis, units, sampling location, or sampling context.

Decision rules:

* YES = publication should be INCLUDED.
* NO = publication should be EXCLUDED because one or more exclusion criteria are clearly met.
* UNCERTAIN = insufficient information is available to determine inclusion or exclusion.

Instructions:

1. Evaluate the title, abstract, and available text.
2. Identify any exclusion criteria that apply.
3. If exclusion is clear, return NO.
4. If inclusion is clear and no exclusion criteria apply, return YES.
5. If evidence is insufficient or ambiguous, return UNCERTAIN.
6. Do not infer information that is not explicitly stated.

Output JSON only:

{
"decision": "YES | NO | UNCERTAIN",
"reason": "brief explanation"
}
